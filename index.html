Where Does the Power Go?

Almost all battery drain is the motor itself, not the cutting. A 1 mm endmill at 30,000 RPM needs about 3W to cut aluminium. But the Speeder motor draws ~90W total to deliver that 3W. Here is why:

3–50 W
Cutting Power
Energy to remove chips. Tiny for small tools in aluminium. Scales with tool size, RPM, and feed rate. See the 6061 table below.
60–200 W
Motor Overhead
The rest. Bearing friction, rotor windage (air drag at 30k+ RPM), motor controller electronics, and motor inefficiency (electrical in > mechanical out). This is the real battery killer.
Example: 1 mm tool at 30,000 RPM — cutting power is ~3W, motor overhead is ~87W, total draw ~90W. That means 97% of battery drain is just keeping the motor spinning, only 3% goes to actually removing material. Even with a 2.5 mm tool at 30k the split is roughly 30% cutting / 70% overhead. The motor has to spin at 30,000+ RPM whether it is cutting or not.
